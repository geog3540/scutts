Choropleth Mapping

In this assignment, I used 2020 Census data to create a choropleth map showing the percent of the population in various Oregon counties with Hispanic or Latino ancestry. The map indicates that counties in eastern and central Oregon have some of the highest percentages of Hispanic and Latino residents. However, not all of the counties in this area share these higher values. Some even exhibit lower percentages of Hispanic and Latino populations than many of the counties in western Oregon. For that reason, it's hard to identify widespread spatial patterns in this data.

Point Symbol Mapping

In this assignment, I used NOAA data to create a proportional symbol map showing the difference in hot spring temperatures across the contiguous United States. The map demonstrates that hot springs are heavily concentrated in the western states, where they also tend to have the highest temperatures. In contrast, hot springs are sparse and generally cooler in the eastern two-thirds of the country.

Bivariate Mapping

In this assignment, I used 2020 Census data to create a bivariate map showing the distribution of Oregon counties by the percentage of residents who are foreign-born and/or of Hispanic or Latino origin. The map indicates that counties with higher percentages of both foreign-born and Hispanic/Latino residents tend to cluster in northern and northeastern Oregon. In contrast, many central and southeastern counties have relatively low percentages of both groups.

Flow Mapping

In this assignment, I used flowmapper.org to create a flow map showing family migration between U.S. states from 1887 to 1924.
